### About me

I have been working with Istio for four years, first as an engineer at Solo.io and now at GEICO. At Solo, I worked on our multi-cluster Istio distribution; now at GEICO, I lead a group of engineers building our custom service-mesh atop of Istio and Envoy running across our on-prem and multi-cloud infrastructure. These experiences have brought me a unique perspective on Istio and its benefits. Working on an Istio-based product at a vendor company, I have seen many enterprises adopt Istio and leverage its features. I am also keenly aware of the difficulty many companies face along the adoption process. Now, at an end-user company, I have a refreshed perspective on the Istio project and the unique challenges that service mesh and zero-trust networking solve in highly-regulated environments. Breadth from a vendor and depth from rolling out our own mesh allow me to understand challenges and perspectives from both backgrounds.


### Why I'm running

In running for the steering committee, I aspire to bring an end-user perspective to the governance of the project. Not a hyperscaler, nor a vendor (not that there's anything wrong with those companies!), but an end-user company trying to practically apply Istio to solve real-world business problems. Vendors and big tech companies have propelled the Istio project to where it is today, and I believe that much of the future growth of Istio lies in continued real-world adoption. Representing the average end-user and sharing their needs and challenges is my goal and commitment. 

I am an active contributor and maintainer to several other projects in the CNCF, but Istio has a special place in my heart. I'd like to help the community and maintainers provide an easy ladder for onboarding new contributors, ensuring a stable and independent future for the project. Istio is a highly-complex project -- which is to be expected; it solves many very hard problems -- but we can make sure that the project remains approachable for small-time contributors and end-users to contribute and engage. Istio has room to improve in this area. Contributing can be overwhelming for newcomers and I've seen it discourage new contributors from seeing their ideas to fruition. Open source sustainability remains an issue across the CNCF; I believe growth of new contributors who eventually grow into mature maintainers is the answer.

Designing and deploying service meshes in many different contexts has shown me that there is no one-size-fits-all approach to a service mesh product.From [introducing beginners to the power of service mesh](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YaMjKL07GDo&t=1s&pp=ygU_c2VjdXJpdHkgYW5kIHNlcnZpY2UgbWVzaCBjbmNmIHNlY3VyaXR5IGNvbmZlcmVuY2UgdHlsZXIgc2NoYWRl) to describing our architecture for [running databases in Istio](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3Jy9VKWgHww&pp=ygUfc2VydmljZSBtZXNoIGFuZCBkYXRhYmFzZXMgY25jZg%3D%3D), I've frequently spoken about what Istio can do for you if you learn to bend it in the right way. I'd like to help document, validate and ensure the stability of "weird" use cases. I believe that this needs to become a priority for the project to adapt to the continued changes in the ecosystem, especially with the adoption of Generative AI. 

Finally, I'm running because working with Istio has been a formative career experience to me, and I want to see the project continue to succeed and grow in the future. It is unreasonable to expect vendors to forever sustain the project, and I hope to help set an example of enterprise engagement and stewardship for critical open-source technologies like Istio. I want to give back to the project, the community, and ensure that the power and flexibiity of Istio remain competitive into the future. I hope to help advocate engagement, shared ownership and do my part to sustain the welcoming Istio community.
